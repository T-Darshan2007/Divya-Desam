About Divya Desam

108 celestial abodes of Lord Vishnu, immortalized in Tamil verse by the Azhwar saints

Sacred Origins

What Are the Divya Desams?

The Divya Desams are 108 temples of Lord Vishnu — Perumal — located across the Indian subcontinent and one in the celestial realm. These sacred shrines are considered divya (divine) because they were sung in devotional praise by the twelve Azhwar saints, mystic poet-saints who lived between the 6th and 9th centuries CE.


The hymns composed by the Azhwars, collectively known as the Nalayira Divya Prabandham (4,000 sacred verses), form the bedrock of Sri Vaishnavism — one of Hinduism's most devotion-rich traditions. Every temple enshrined in these hymns carries the spiritual vibration of the saint's ecstatic experience of the divine.

The Twelve Mystic Poets

The Azhwar Saints

The word Azhwar means "one who is immersed in God." These twelve saint-poets experienced transcendent unity with Lord Vishnu and channelled that bliss into immortal Tamil verses. Their hymns sanctified the 108 temples, giving each shrine its "divya desam" status.

Why These Temples Matter

Sacred Significance

I
Divine Validation

A temple is classified as a Divya Desam only if it was sung by one of the twelve Azhwars in the Nalayira Divya Prabandham. This poetic canon acts as the supreme authentication of a temple's divinity within the Sri Vaishnava tradition.

II
Geographic Span

105 temples are in India, 1 in Nepal (Muktinath), 1 in the celestial world (Thiruppaarkadal), and 1 in the soul (Thiruppati). Together they span Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala, Uttar Pradesh and beyond.

III
Architectural Heritage

Many Divya Desam temples are masterpieces of Dravidian architecture, with towering gopurams (gateway towers), intricate sculptures, and sacred tanks that reflect centuries of patronage by Chola, Pandya and Vijayanagara kings.

IV
Living Tradition

Daily rituals, seasonal festivals and the recitation of the Divya Prabandham continue unbroken for over a thousand years. Pilgrims circumambulate all 108 temples — called Divya Desam Yatra — a lifelong spiritual aspiration for millions of devotees.
